與前端工程師合作建構的多產品線內容管理平台，讓行銷與業管人員無需設計師介入即可產出符合品牌規範的活動頁面。這套架構後續成為集團其他後台系統開發的設計藍本。 A multi-product-line CMS built with one frontend engineer, enabling marketing and operations teams to publish brand-consistent campaign pages without designer involvement. 背景Context Context背景

公司情境 Company situation

永慶房屋為全台市佔第一的房仲集團，集團底下橫跨多個業務子品牌——估價、資產管理、知識內容、店官網、代銷、共享資訊等——每條產品線都需要持續產出活動頁、品牌頁、宣傳頁面。

在 CMS 上線之前，這些頁面的產出全都依賴設計師逐一手工製作，形成一個典型的設計師瓶頸：行銷與業管團隊有頁面需求，但要排隊等設計與前端資源。

Yungching is Taiwan's #1 real estate group, with multiple business sub-brands—appraisal, asset management, knowledge content, agent storefronts, agency, and shared information services. Each product line continuously produces campaign, brand, and promotional pages.

Before the CMS, every page required hand-crafted work by designers, creating a classic designer bottleneck: marketing and operations teams had page needs but had to queue for design and engineering resources.

設計挑戰 The design challenge

挑戰並不只是「做一個 CMS」——而是設計一套非設計師也能正確使用的設計系統。系統必須夠彈性，讓內容方擁有組合自由度；又必須夠約束，確保產出的每一個頁面都符合品牌規範。

同時這套系統要同時服務 8 條業務性質迥異的產品線，每條線的 SEO 需求、生命週期、發布流程都略有不同。

The challenge wasn't simply "build a CMS"—it was to design a design system that non-designers can use correctly. Flexible enough to give content producers compositional freedom, yet constrained enough to ensure every output page meets brand standards.

It also had to serve 8 product lines with distinct business needs—each with slightly different SEO requirements, content lifecycles, and publishing workflows.

D · 01

元件化區塊編輯器Component-based block editor Component-based block editor元件化區塊編輯器

問題 Problem

內容方需要組合自由度，但若給予完全自由的編輯器，輸出品質將失控；若給予完全固定的模板，又無法滿足多元的內容需求。

Content producers need compositional freedom, but a fully open editor leads to inconsistent output. A fully locked template can't satisfy diverse content needs.

設計決策 Design decision

將品牌頁面拆解為三大類元件——標題文件、文章元件、按鈕元件——每類提供多種預先設計好的版型（Slider、大圖、並排列表、小圖列表等）。內容方透過拖拉組合既有元件來組頁，自由度與一致性同時被守住。這個架構等同於 Notion／Webflow 的區塊編輯器，但專為房仲業務內容設計。

I deconstructed brand pages into three component categories—headers, article blocks, and CTA buttons—each offering multiple pre-designed variants (Slider, large image, side-by-side list, small image list, etc.). Content producers compose pages by dragging existing components, preserving both freedom and consistency. The architecture mirrors Notion/Webflow's block editor, designed specifically for real estate content.

內嵌 SEO 預覽Inline SEO preview Inline SEO preview內嵌 SEO 預覽

問題 Problem

行銷人員不一定理解網頁標題、描述、縮圖會在 Google／Facebook／LINE 上呈現的樣子，常導致 SEO 與分享效果不佳，但設計師難以一一說明。

Marketing staff don't always understand how page titles, descriptions, and thumbnails render on Google, Facebook, or LINE—leading to poor SEO and sharing performance. But designers can't explain this case-by-case.

設計決策 Design decision

在編輯欄位旁即時顯示搜尋結果與分享預覽，搭配「不限字數，建議 20 個中文字以內」這類「不強制但給建議」的微文案。讓使用者不需要設計師指導也能寫出有效的 metadata，並理解每個欄位真正影響的是什麼。

I embedded live previews of search and share results next to each field, paired with non-coercive guidance microcopy like "no character limit, but 20 Chinese characters recommended." Users can write effective metadata without designer guidance, and understand what each field actually affects.

D · 03

內容生命週期管理Content lifecycle management Content lifecycle management內容生命週期管理

問題 Problem

活動頁不是發布即結束——它有上架時間、下架時間、編輯協作、版本管理等真實工作流。傳統 CMS 把這些當作次要功能，造成行銷團隊額外的協調負擔。

A campaign page isn't done at publish—it has scheduled launch and takedown, collaborative editing, and version management. Traditional CMS treats these as secondary, creating extra coordination burden for marketing teams.

設計決策 Design decision

把「上架／下架時間預排」「頁面複製」「暫存草稿」這些貼合行銷實際工作流的功能，設計成第一級的操作。長表單的「下一步／暫存草稿／取消」按鈕在頁首與頁尾各放一次，避免長頁面操作疲勞。這些都是「懂使用者真實工作流」的 enterprise B 端細節。

I designed scheduled publish/unpublish, page duplication, and draft autosave as first-class actions—matching marketing's real workflow. The "Next / Save draft / Cancel" buttons appear at both top and bottom of long forms to reduce operation fatigue. These are the enterprise B2B details that signal "designed by someone who understands real users."

這個 CMS 不是一次性的專案，而是一個會被沿用、被複製、會繼續長下去的架構。這是我四年後在 Figma 元件庫上重複的同一個工作模式——把工作做成系統，而不是專案。 This CMS wasn't a one-off project—it became an architecture that gets reused, copied, and continues to grow. The same working pattern I'd repeat four years later with the Figma component library: build systems, not projects.

回顧Reflection Reflection回顧

這個案例對我的意義，遠超過它在永慶內部的影響範圍。

在 2021 年，我還沒有意識到自己天生用「系統」而非「專案」在思考設計。我只是覺得：「如果這個架構做得好，後面的人會比較輕鬆。」

四年後，當我主導永慶房仲網的買屋頻道改版、建立 Figma 元件庫、再延伸到租屋／知識／社區頻道時，我才回頭看見——這是同一個我，做了同一件事，只是換了不同的工具與規模。

真正讓設計師值錢的，不是做了多少漂亮的介面，而是留下多少會繼續長下去的架構。

This case study means more to me than its internal impact at Yungching.

In 2021, I hadn't yet realized that I think in systems, not projects. I just thought: "If this architecture is well-made, the people who come after will have an easier time."

Four years later, when I led the Yungching real estate site's "Buy" channel redesign, built the Figma component library, and extended it to "Rent / Knowledge / Community" channels—I looked back and saw: same person, same act, different tools and scale.

What makes a designer truly valuable isn't how many beautiful interfaces they ship—it's how many architectures they leave behind that continue to grow.
